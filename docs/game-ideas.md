Bubble Pop: Educational version with sorting, counting, and letters/numbers.

Digital Coloring: This should be a multi touch capable paint. where you can selet from a handful of colors and then paint on the screen

Peek-a-Boo: Simple interaction to reveal animals and toys.

Scratcher: Swipe to reveal hidden animals like cats or dogs.

Ball Launch: Tapping to trigger physics-based launches at targets.

Sound Board: Tapping shapes to trigger musical or animal sounds.

Shape Sorter: Dragging shapes to match silhouettes.

Flashlight: Moving a beam of light to uncover hidden items in the dark.


toddler specific thoughts for the games above
Bubble Pop: Increase the touch radius of each bubble significantly so he doesn't have to be pixel-perfect. When a target (like the cow) is popped, have it "burst" into smaller, harmless glitter particles rather than just disappearing.

Digital Coloring: large color selection and tool selection buttons.

Peek-a-Boo: Use a "spring" animation for the door opening. When it swings open, have the animal bounce slightly or wave. Adding a short, distinct sound (a "boing" for the door, then the animal sound) makes the sequence feel rewarding.

Scratcher: To prevent frustration, keep the required "scratch" percentage low (e.g., 30% of the surface area removed triggers the transition). Once it’s cleared, automatically trigger a clean wipe animation (like a camera flash) to reset for the next image. This should work like a lottery ticket where the image is completely covered initially with a dark gray covering. Then as you swipe over it with your finger, that covering is "scratched off" so that you can see the animal on the layer beneath it.

Ball Launch: Use a "tap-to-fire" mechanic rather than dragging. When he taps the launch button, have the ball travel in a gentle arc. Even if he misses the target, add a subtle "magnet" effect that nudges the ball toward the closest target so he almost always gets a satisfying ding. There should be targets and hoops. The ball should launch from the side of the screen toward where a finger taps a target. The launcher should be slideable like if I touch and drag it should go up and down to adjust its height. Then when I tap somewhere on the screen the ball should launch toward where I tapped.

Sound Board: Make these full-screen icons. Use large, distinct shapes (Circle, Square, Triangle) in primary colors. When touched, the shape should grow in size momentarily and play a pleasant, non-jarring sound (like a chime or a low-register "moo").

Shape Sorter: Use a "snapping" mechanism. As the shape gets near its shadow, have it visually highlight or "glow" to let him know it’s in the right zone, then automatically pull it into place with a successful sound. This should work without having any images. All the shapes should just be rendereded to reduce the amount of images.

Flashlight: Limit the "beam" radius so the objects aren't too obscured, which can cause anxiety in toddlers. When the beam passes over an object, have the object light up and emit a sound—this reinforces the "discovery" aspect.
